Learning Plan

Long-Term Goals and Career Pathway

My long-term goal is to become a Full Stack Web Developer and eventually transition into a Team Lead role, where I can manage a small team focused on developing new tools and technologies. I want to grow not only as a developer but also as a leader, helping guide a team through challenges and shaping innovative projects. Leadership is something I aim to cultivate, and I'm excited about balancing the technical side of things with managing people and processes.

Strengths and Limitations in Learning

One of my biggest strengths is curiosity. I have a natural drive to explore new topics and dive deep into anything that piques my interest. This sense of curiosity has always kept me motivated, especially when working through unfamiliar or challenging material. I find joy in digging deeper into subjects, which often leads me to valuable insights or solutions that I might not have initially considered.

However, a limitation I've recognized in myself is a lack of discipline. While curiosity drives me, it can also distract me. I sometimes struggle with starting tasks, especially when they are repetitive or feel less exciting. When I lose focus, it's often because I'm drawn toward new experiences or material instead of staying consistent with the fundamentals. I believe that through Bootcamp, I can develop the discipline I need by practicing consistency and maintaining focus on both exciting and routine tasks.

Non-Technical Skills I Want to Develop

One of the human skills I'd like to focus on during my time at Dev Academy is improving my communication, particularly when it comes to teamwork. I want to become more effective at articulating my ideas clearly and collaborating smoothly with others, both in problem-solving and general interactions.

Additionally, I want to strengthen my discipline. This ties into my limitation around focus and consistency, and I believe that a structured environment like Bootcamp will help me develop the habits necessary to manage my time and tasks efficiently.

Managing My Workload

To manage my workload effectively, I commit to being open and respectful in my communications with other learners, facilitators, and industry representatives. Active participation in group activities will be a priority for me, offering constructive feedback and being receptive to the ideas of others. I plan to uphold professional standards in all my interactions, ensuring mutual respect and maintaining a safe and productive learning environment.

I will begin each week by setting clear goals and creating a structured schedule, prioritizing tasks based on how familiar or challenging I find the subject. When I start to feel overwhelmed, my approach will be to break down tasks into smaller, manageable steps and seek guidance from peers or facilitators as needed. Incorporating regular breaks and practicing self-care will also be key to managing stress and staying productive.

Seeking Help in a Timely Manner

In terms of seeking help, I'm confident that I can utilize our well-structured Discord channels to ask questions or get support. The online development community is an incredible resource that I plan to tap into regularly. I will continue to use search engines and online forums to find solutions when I hit roadblocks. Expectations from the Facilitation Team

I expect the Facilitation team to play a key role in mentoring and helping me stay on track. I tend to get lost in the details of my work sometimes, so I hope the facilitators can guide me in distinguishing what features or elements are truly valuable and when I'm overemphasizing something less important. This kind of feedback will help me balance my perfectionism with the realities of project timelines and deliverables.

Additionally, I'm looking for the team to provide opportunities for connecting with the broader IT community. Being able to network and engage with real-world opportunities will be invaluable, both for learning and for potential employment prospects.

Scheduling Commitments

At the moment, I have no significant scheduling conflicts. I'm wrapping up two part-time jobs by the beginning of October, just in time for Bootcamp. Moving forward, I'll only be working on a small part-time website project that requires limited hours each week. This will allow me to focus nearly all my energy on Bootcamp, ensuring I can commit fully to my learning and growth.
